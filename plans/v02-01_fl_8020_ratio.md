# v02-01 — FL-aligned 80:20 zero-shot evaluation under PFL framing

> Successor to `v01-01_peak_from_latent_test.md`. Scoped down from the
> earlier v02 sweep proposal: single 80:20 split, zero-shot inference only,
> with routing ablation. K-shot personalization is split off into v03.

## Motivation

v01 reports cold PAPE −18% / −32% on a **50:50 train:cold** UMass split,
with two FL-related caveats it disclosed honestly:

1. **§ 1.3 / § D.6.1** — "is not a federated training algorithm; codebook
   is a post-hoc artifact"; the FedAvg + post-hoc Peak-VQ extension is
   listed as P3 future work.
2. **§ 5.4.6** — "50 cold households is moderate; no per-household power
   analysis"; 50:50 is also an outlier vs. FL/cold-start literature
   (BuildingsBench ≈ 99.8 : 0.2, FedCCL 80:20, Briggs et al. 80:20-style
   folds, etc.).

v02 closes the second caveat directly and reframes the first.

### PFL framing (new in v02)

Recast v01's centralized backbone as a **frozen shared encoder** that is
*pretrained centrally on donor/historical data and then frozen* — the
FedHiP pattern (arxiv:2508.04470). Under this view:

- Backbone training is **not** federated and does not need to be — the
  encoder is a public artifact (or a one-time pretrain over consenting
  donors).
- The only object the federation produces is the **post-hoc codebook**
  (centroid + offset).
- At inference time, each cold gucha calls the shared backbone *locally*
  to obtain its `h_g_cold` and looks up the global codebook *locally*. No
  raw load, no hidden vector, no input statistic leaves the cold gucha.

This makes v01 §5.3's privacy claim coherent: KEY is a local computation,
VALUE (centroid + offset) is k-anonymous server broadcast, and there is
no cold-side training that could leak gradients.

### Why `h_g` aggregation is acceptable for v02

v02 keeps **Option X** from the brainstorm: train-side hidden vectors
`{h_g^{(j)}}` are aggregated centrally for the one-shot KMeans fit. Two
points justify this for v02 scope:

- The aggregation happens **at the donor / pretraining stage**, not at
  inference. By the PFL framing above, this is the same threat surface
  as the backbone pretrain — accepted in FedHiP-style designs.
- The 1-shot post-hoc KMeans dodges the in-loop TAR attack
  (arxiv:2511.07073, 43–77% input reconstruction from iterative centroid
  release). Privacy at the aggregation step is a separate question
  (model inversion of 64-d hidden), tracked as v04 future work.

## Goals

**G1.** Re-run the full v01 pipeline at **single 80:20 split** (n_train=80,
n_cold=20) on UMass and report whether:
- the headline cold PAPE improvement (−18% HR-preserving / −32%
  PAPE-aggressive) survives;
- the +18.6 pp peak_aux V0 ablation effect (E1) survives;
- the codebook health metric (k_min ≥ 113 at M=32) survives at the
  larger train pool.

**G2.** Routing-mechanism ablation: **R0** (v01's 5-d KEY-NN) vs **R1**
(64-d `h_g_cold` direct nearest-centroid). Under the PFL framing the cold
gucha already runs the backbone forward, so R1 incurs zero extra cost
and uses ×12 more information per query. Quantify ΔPAPE / ΔHR@k.

**G3.** Multi-seed stability (3 seeds, matching v01 §4.4 protocol).

**G4.** **W-component decomposition at 80:20** (new in v02): hold the
backbone fixed at T2 and compare {V0-only, W1a-only, W5-hybrid} on R0
routing. v01 §4.6 iter4 established W5 dominance on the 50:50 split; v02
asks whether that ranking survives the larger train pool / smaller cold
pool. Implemented in `06_W_component_ablation.py`, orthogonal to G1's E1
(which holds mechanism = V0 fixed and varies the backbone).

## Non-goals (deferred)

- **K-shot adaptation** (F2a/F2b/F2c) → **v03**.
- **Whole backbone fine-tune** (F1) → v04.
- **Federated KMeans / DP-KMeans** → v04+.
- **Second dataset** (LCL, Pecan Street) → v04+.
- **Other forecasting baselines** (DLinear, NHiTS, Crossformer, Chronos, …) → v04+. v01 used the NBEATSx family throughout; v02 keeps the model frozen so the comparison stays "v01 50:50 vs v02 80:20" on identical method.
- **Hyperparameter re-tuning** of (σ, α_v0, α_w1) — carry over v01's two
  operating points unchanged. Cold-split tuning is a v01 §5.4.1 concern;
  introducing it here would compound that issue.

## Method (delta from v01 only)

Everything in v01 §3 is reused unchanged except:

| Component | v01 | v02 |
|---|---|---|
| Split | 50 train : 50 cold | **80 train : 20 cold** |
| Backbone framing | "centralized" | "frozen shared encoder (PFL)" — implementation identical |
| Codebook routing | R0 only (5-d KEY-NN) | **R0 + R1 ablation** (R1 = 64-d `h_g_cold` to centroid distance) |
| Cold inference | warm-start z-norm + zero-shot | unchanged |
| Operating points | HR-preserving, PAPE-aggressive | unchanged |
| Seeds | 3 | 3 |

**R1 implementation**: at cold inference, compute `h_g_cold` (already
required for W5's auxiliary head), then `c* = argmin_c ‖h_g_cold −
codebook[c]‖₂`. No backbone modification, no extra forward pass.

## Experimental plan

### Splits

`outputs/v02_fl_8020_ratio/splits/v02_8020.yaml` — extend v10
stratification to cut at 80:20 with the same random_state. Single split,
no rotation.

### Per-seed pipeline

For each seed ∈ {42, 123, 7} (one invocation per seed via `--seed S`):

1. **`02_train_arms.py`** — train T0 (no peak_aux) and T2 (with peak_aux) at 80:20.
2. **`03_fit_codebook.py`** — fit M=32 KMeans codebook on T2 latents; save centroids + per-cluster offsets + KEY pool + scaler params.
3. **`04_coldstart_eval.py`** — cold inference at both operating points × {R0, R1} → 4 cells per seed (G1 + G2).
4. **`05_E1_ablation.py`** — E1 ablation (V0 mechanism only) — peak_aux ON/OFF, mirrors v01 §4.3 (G1 +18.6 pp check).
5. **`06_W_component_ablation.py`** — W-component decomposition (T2 × {V0, W1a, W5}, R0 routing) — G4.

### Metrics

Identical to v01 §4.1: PAPE (kW, denormalised), HR@1, HR@2, codebook
{k_min, utilization, perplexity}.

## Open questions

1. **Stratification at 80:20**: with cold n=20, does v10's KMeans-based
   stratifier still produce a representative cold set, or does the small
   cold size make assignment variance dominate? Possible mitigation:
   3-fold cold rotation (run 3 different cold-20 sets, report all).
   Decide before kickoff.

2. **R1 metric**: `‖h_g_cold − centroid‖₂` is plain Euclidean. Should it
   use StandardScaler-normalised distance (matching v01's KEY-NN
   normalisation) or raw? Default to raw; if that underperforms R0
   despite ×12 information, try normalised.

3. **Cold n=20 statistical power**: 3 seeds × 20 cold gucha × ~240
   windows ≈ 14K cold windows per seed. Should still keep PAPE σ < 0.5,
   but verify in dry-run.
